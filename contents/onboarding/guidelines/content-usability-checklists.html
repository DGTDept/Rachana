Interface design is much more than the arrangement of text and components to create an aesthetically pleasing digital product. It is about presenting users with appropriate visual cues and information to enable them to efficiently and effortlessly accomplish their goals

Informational Services

The checklist consists of 27 points that are broadly categorised into 5 sections, namely: (i) Homepage, (ii) Accessibility, (iii) Navigation, (iv) Content, (v) Performance and User Experience. Each point has three ratings: Pass, Consider Fixing (Usability violation but not critical), and Should fix (High impact violations. Should be fixed immediately).

This checklist is not:

  • an evaluation of the agency’s e-services and its processes
  • an evaluation of aesthetics, though a minimalist approach is always preferred
  • recommendations on business strategies, colour palette, typefaces or interface design trends
  • a replacement for user testing. Usability testing is a very important assessment method that will uncover issues which might not fall within the scope of this checklist.

Transactional Services

The list consists of 30 points that are broadly categorised into six sections, namely (i) homepage, (ii) accessibility, (iii) navigation, (iv) content, (v) forms and (vi) performance and user experience. Each point has three basic ratings: Green = Good/Pass, Amber = Consider Fixing, but no disaster, and Red = Needs fixing/Fail.

This checklist is not:

  • an evaluation of the agency’s e-services and its processes
  • an evaluation of aesthetics, though a minimalist approach is always preferred
  • recommendations on business strategies, colour palette, typefaces or interface design trends
  • a replacement for user testing. Usability testing is a very important assessment method that will uncover issues which might not fall within the scope of this checklist.
